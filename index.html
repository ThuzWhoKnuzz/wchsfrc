Inspire

Turning curiosity into confidence.

We don’t expect members to join with years of experience. We expect them to show up curious. From the first week, new students get hands-on with tools, code, and robots in a safe, mentored environment.

  • Onboarding labs. Short build and code challenges that take an idea from sketch to working prototype in a single meeting.
  • Mini-lessons. Officers and mentors teach topics like “how to read a wiring diagram” or “what PID actually does” in 10–15 minutes.
  • Shadow days. New members sit with an experienced student during real work sessions to see how the team runs.

Engineer

From whiteboard sketch to competition robot.

Our build process is structured like a real engineering pipeline. Every week, the robot moves further along the path from idea → CAD → prototype → final system.

Week 1 · Game Breakdown 0%

Strategy meeting, whiteboard sketches, and deciding what our robot will and won’t do on the field.

Weeks 2–3 · CAD & Prototyping 0%

CAD models, cardboard mockups, and rapid-fire prototypes to decide on intakes, shooters, and drivetrains.

Weeks 4–5 · Build & Wiring 0%

CNC parts, assembly, clean electrical layout, and first full-robot power-on tests.

Weeks 6+ · Tuning & Drive Practice 0%

Autonomous routines, vision tuning, driver practice, and “break it now so it won’t break on the field.”

Collaborate

Subteams that move as one robot.

Each subteam owns a part of the robot — but nothing ships unless everyone is in sync. Weekly syncs, shared documentation, and cross-training keep the whole machine moving.

Mechanical

Designs and builds the structure, manipulators, and mechanisms that interact with game pieces.

  • CAD & fabrication
  • Drivebase & superstructure
  • Pit repair crew at competition

Programming

Writes the code that turns metal and motors into a responsive, smart robot.

  • Autonomous routines
  • Driver controls & tuning
  • Vision & sensor fusion

Electrical

Owns power, wiring, and reliability so the robot survives every match.

  • Clean CAN & power routing
  • Labeling & documentation
  • Between-match diagnostics

Outreach & Media

Builds the story around the robot — sponsors, community events, and branding.

  • Sponsorship outreach
  • Social media & branding
  • STEM events and demos

Compete

Where all the late nights pay off.

Competition is where we stress-test everything: our robot, our systems, and our teamwork. We show up early, stay late, and treat every match as a data point.
